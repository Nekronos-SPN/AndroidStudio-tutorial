In this section, you'll take your initial steps in building an Android app using Android Studio. The focus is on creating a new project, navigating through the development environment, and understanding the layout structure. You'll explore how to run your app on both an emulator and a physical device, gaining hands-on experience with the essential tools provided by Android Studio. By the end of this section, you'll have a solid foundation for developing your Android applications.

## Step 1: Create a New Project

1. Open Android Studio.
2. Click "Start a new Android Studio project" in the Welcome dialog.
3. Select "Basic Activity" and name your application (e.g., My First App).
4. Ensure the language is set to Java and click "Finish."

## Step 2: Create a Virtual Device (Emulator)
:::tip[Performance]
If your experience very slow performance during the execution of the following steps, you can skip to [step 4](#step-4-run-your-app-on-a-physical-device).
:::

1. Open the AVD Manager in Android Studio.
2. Click "Create Virtual Device" and choose a device definition (e.g., Pixel 2).
3. Select the latest system image, download if necessary, and complete the setup.

### Step 2.1: Run Your App on the Emulator


1. Click "Run app" in Android Studio or use the toolbar icon.
2. Choose your virtual device in the deployment target dialog.
3. Android Studio will build, upload, and run your app on the emulator.

## Step 4: Run Your App on a Physical Device

> Prerequisites:
> - An Android device (phone or tablet).
> - A data cable to connect your device to your computer.
> - [Android Debugging Bridge](https://developer.android.com/tools/adb) installed on you computer.


1. Enable [Developer Options and USB Debugging](https://developer.android.com/studio/debug/dev-options).
2. Connect your device to your computer via USB.
3. Click "Run 'app'" and choose your connected device.
